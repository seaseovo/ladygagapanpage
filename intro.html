Introduction

Lady Gaga, born Stefani Joanne Angelina Germanotta, is an American singer, songwriter, and actress known for her versatility in entertainment. She began her career as a teenager, singing and acting in school plays. Gaga's debut studio album, The Fame, was released in 2007, and her five subsequent albums all debuted atop the US Billboard 200. Her second full-length album, Born This Way, explored electronic rock and techno-pop, and her third album, Artpop, sold over one million copies in its first week. Gaga also gained recognition for her acting roles in American Horror Story: Hotel and A Star Is Born, winning multiple awards. Her sixth studio album, Chromatica, released in 2020, and she starred in the biopic House of Gucci in 2021. Gaga has sold an estimated 170 million records and is one of the world's best-selling music artists.

Life & Career

  • 1986–2004: Early life

    • Stefani Joanne Angelina Germanotta, born in Manhattan in 1986, is a singer and actress known for her music career. Born to Italian parents, she attended the Convent of the Sacred Heart and later studied at the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute. Gaga began playing the piano at four and was encouraged by her parents to pursue music. She played lead roles in plays like Guys and Dolls and A Funny Thing Happened on the Way to the Forum at Regis High School. Gaga studied method acting at the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute for ten years. In 2003, she gained early admission to Collaborative Arts Project 21, a music school at New York University's Tisch School of the Arts. In 2005, she withdrew from school to focus on her music career and played an unsuspecting diner customer on MTV's Boiling Points. In 2014, Gaga revealed that she was raped at age 19 by her producer and underwent mental and physical therapy. She has post-traumatic stress disorder (PTSD) and credits support from doctors, family, and friends for helping her.

  • 2005–2007: Career beginnings

    • Lady Gaga, the popular singer and actress, began her career in 2005 with the audio book "The Portal in the Park" and formed the band SGBand. She was recommended to music producer Rob Fusari by Wendy Starland, who helped her develop her songs and compose new material. They formed a company called "Team Lovechild, LLC" to promote her career, and Gaga was signed to Def Jam Recordings in 2006. Gaga began incorporating pop melodies and the glam rock style of David Bowie and Queen into her songs. In 2007, she signed with Vincent Herbert, who later signed her to his label Streamline Records. Gaga also signed with Interscope Geffen A&M Records, where she was made his "franchise player." In 2007, she collaborated with songwriter RedOne on her debut album, Cherrytree Records, and wrote four songs with him. Despite securing a record deal, Gaga said that some radio stations found her music too "racy", "dance-oriented", and "underground" for the mainstream market.

  • 2008–2010: Breakthrough with The Fame and The Fame Monster

    • Lady Gaga's debut album, The Fame, was released in 2008 and reached number one in various countries, including Austria, Canada, Germany, Ireland, Switzerland, and the UK. The album's first two singles, "Just Dance" and "Poker Face," were the world's best-selling singles of 2009, with 9.8 million copies sold. Gaga also released three other singles, including "Eh, Eh (Nothing Else I Can Say)", "LoveGame", and "Paparazzi". The Fame and "Poker Face" won Best Dance/Electronica Album and Best Dance Recording at the 52nd Annual Grammy Awards.

    In 2010, Gaga headlined The Monster Ball Tour, which grossed $227 million and became the highest-grossing concert tour for a debut headlining artist. She wrote eight songs for The Fame Monster, a reissue of The Fame, and released a standalone EP. The Fame Monster won the Grammy Award for Best Pop Vocal Album and "Bad Romance" won Best Female Pop Vocal Performance and Best Short Form Music Video at the 53rd Annual Grammy Awards.

  • 2011–2014: Born This Way, Artpop, and Cheek to Cheek

    • In 2011, Lady Gaga released "Born This Way," the lead single from her studio album, which debuted atop the Billboard Hot 100 and became the 1,000th number-one single in history. The album sold eight million copies worldwide and received three Grammy nominations, including Gaga's third consecutive nomination for Album of the Year.

    Gaga also worked with Tony Bennett on a jazz version of "The Lady Is a Tramp", Elton John on "Hello Hello" for the animated feature film Gnomeo & Juliet, and with The Lonely Island and Justin Timberlake on "3-Way (The Golden Rule)". She performed a concert at the Sydney Town Hall in Australia to promote Born This Way and celebrate former US President Bill Clinton's 65th birthday.

    In 2011, Gaga began work on her third studio album, Artpop, during the Born This Way Ball tour. The album debuted atop the Billboard 200 chart and sold more than 2.5 million copies worldwide as of July 2014. The album's lead single, "Applause," reached number one in Hungary, number four in the US, and number five in the UK.

    During the Cheek to Cheek era, Gaga overhauled her image, hosting an episode of Saturday Night Live in November 2013. She performed a special rendition of "Do What U Want" with Christina Aguilera on The Voice and embarked on the ArtRave: The Artpop Ball tour, earning $83 million.

  • 2018–2019: A Star Is Born and Las Vegas residency

    • In 2015, Gaga reinvented her image and style with the release of Cheek to Cheek and her performance at the 87th Academy Awards. She co-wrote the song "Til It Happens to You" for the documentary The Hunting Ground, earning them the Satellite Award for Best Original Song and an Academy Award nomination. Gaga won Billboard Woman of the Year and Contemporary Icon Award at the 2015 Annual Songwriters Hall of Fame Awards.

    In 2016, Gaga starred in American Horror Story: Hotel, the fifth season of the television anthology horror series. She received the Best Actress in a Miniseries or Television Film award at the 73rd Golden Globe Awards. She also appeared in Nick Knight's 2015 fashion film for Tom Ford's 2016 spring campaign and was guest editor for V fashion magazine's 99th issue in January 2016.

    In 2017, Gaga performed as the headlining act during the Super Bowl LI halftime show, featuring a group of hundreds of lighted drones forming various shapes in the sky above Houston's NRG Stadium. The performance attracted 117.5 million viewers in the United States and earned her an Emmy nomination in the Outstanding Special Class Program category.

    In 2018, Gaga's chronic pain, fibromyalgia, led to the cancellation of the Joanne World Tour, which grossed $95 million from 842,000 tickets sold.

  • 2020–present: Chromatica, Love for Sale, and further film projects

    • In February 2020, Gaga released her sixth studio album, Chromatica, in May 2020. The album debuted at number one in the US and reached the top spot in over a dozen other territories. Gaga won five awards at the 2020 MTV Video Music Awards, including the inaugural Tricon Award. In September 2020, she appeared in a video campaign for Valentino's Voce Viva fragrance, singing a stripped-down version of Chromatica track "Sine from Above".

    In 2022, Gaga performed on The Chromatica Ball, her first all-stadium concert tour. She sang the US national anthem during Joe Biden's inauguration and was hospitalized after her dog walker Ryan Fischer was shot in Hollywood. Gaga offered a $500,000 reward for the return of her pets, but the man who shot Fischer was sentenced to 21 years in prison.

    In April 2021, Gaga teamed up with Champagne brand Dom Pérignon and released her third remix album, Dawn of Chromatica. She also released her second collaborative album, Love for Sale, with Tony Bennett, which received generally favorable reviews and debuted at number eight in the US. Gaga co-wrote the song "Hold My Hand" for Top Gun: Maverick and performed it live at the 95th Academy Awards.

    In April 2023, Gaga was appointed as co-chair of the President's Committee on the Arts and Humanities by President Joe Biden.
